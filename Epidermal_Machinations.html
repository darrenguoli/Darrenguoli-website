This project (ongoing) aims to reconceptualize skin as a dynamic interface for the human condition through a series of oil paintings, video, and photographic work. Skin is the nexus of our internal (physiology, emotions, and heritage) and external (social and environmental) domains. The project visually dissects the physical properties of skin (e.g. coloration, elasticity, texture, temporality) and illustrates its role as an essential channel for the transmission of stimuli to and from our bodies. What really gives someone goosebumps or wrinkles; what makes someone blush, sweat, glow; what shocks someone into pallor? We cannot consciously control the physiological responses of our skin to stimuli and thereby the project will aim to access skin as a direct line to the subconscious. While the paintings concentrate on the skin’s characteristics, the video and photographic work will investigate skin’s reactions to external stimuli. On the human-human level we are hyper-sensitive, self-conscious, and yet social beings with our unique ticks to certain impetuses. In the bigger picture, we are amidst a mass biodiversity extinction, on-going genocide, and climate crisis. Can skin elucidate what people really think about social discourses and global crises?
